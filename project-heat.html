3D Heat Diffusion & Jacobi Relaxation

Physical setup: a \(10 \times 10 \times 10\) insulated 3D lattice with one hot voxel placed near the center. Everything else starts at 0 °C. Heat spreads by six-way conduction while the source is held at 200 °C, producing a clear transient heat plume in the animation.

Math background: explicit finite-difference diffusion on a unit grid, \( \partial_t T = \nabla^2 T \), which is stable for \( \Delta t < 1/6 \) in 3D. The same discretization also acts as a Jacobi relaxation demo: splitting the Laplacian system as \(A = D + L + U\) and updating \(x^{k+1} = D^{-1}(b - (L+U)x^k)\) with over-relaxation. I track convergence using the infinity norm of successive iterates.

This model serves as a clean benchmark for Poisson and heat-equation solvers.
