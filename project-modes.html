Coupled Spring Normal Modes

Physical setup: eight equal masses connected by springs along a fixed rail. Displacements stay small, so each mass oscillates around equilibrium while forces travel down the chain, forming standing-wave patterns across the system.

Math background: assembling the stiffnessâ€“mass matrix \(A\) with \(A_{ii} = (k_i + k_{i+1})/m_i\) and \(A_{i,i\pm1} = -k_{i\pm1}/m_i\) reduces the equations of motion to the eigenproblem \(A q = \omega^2 q\). Eigenvalues give squared frequencies, eigenvectors give mode shapes, and each mode is animated as \(q \sin(\omega t)\) to show phase and relative amplitude.

Normal modes dictate how real structures vibrate, where nodes form, and how energy distributes across a system.
