Multivariate Newton-Raphson Basins

Physical system: trajectories in the \((x,y)\) plane as a two-variable nonlinear system relaxes toward its roots. Starting seeds are scattered around the domain so each basin funnels a different path down to a shared surface.

Math: Newton updates \(x_{k+1} = x_k - J^{-1}(x_k)F(x_k)\) for \(F = [2x^3 - 6xy^2 - 1,\; 6x^2y - 2y^3 + 3]\). Inverting the Jacobian yields step directions; I halve the step for smoother motion and log \( \lVert F \rVert_2 \) to build a convergence landscape. Cubic interpolation fills in trajectories for the animation.

Why convergence wiggles: near-singular Jacobians flatten directions so steps overshoot; seeds that start across basin boundaries bounce between attractors; and damping the step slows otherwise quadratic convergence into a more linear crawl until the iterates get close to a root.

Why it matters: basin structure tells you how sensitive a solver is to initialization; shallow valleys and singular Jacobians flag where line searches or trust regions are needed before deploying Newton in higher dimensions.
